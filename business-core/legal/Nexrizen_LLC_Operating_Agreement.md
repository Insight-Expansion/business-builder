## RECITALS

The Members desire to enter into this Agreement to define the rights, duties, and obligations of the Members and to govern the affairs of the Company.

---

## ARTICLE IV — PROFITS, LOSSES, AND DISTRIBUTIONS

**4.1 Allocation of Profits and Losses.** The net profits and net losses of the Company shall be allocated to the Members in proportion to their respective ownership interests as set forth in Section 2.1.

**4.2 Distributions.** Distributions of available cash or other assets shall be made to the Members in proportion to their respective ownership interests, at such times and in such amounts as determined by the unanimous consent of the Managing Members, subject to applicable law and the Company's need to retain funds for operations.

**4.3 Tax Allocations.** For federal and Puerto Rico income tax purposes, items of income, gain, loss, deduction, and credit shall be allocated among the Members in accordance with their respective ownership interests, unless otherwise required by applicable tax law.

---

## ARTICLE V — MANAGEMENT

**5.1 Member-Managed.** The Company shall be managed by its Members (a "member-managed" LLC). Both Members shall serve as Managing Members and shall have equal authority to bind the Company in the ordinary course of business.

**5.2 Duties of Managing Members.**

(a) **Wei Zhuo (CEO)** — Shall be responsible for strategic direction, technology leadership, business development, and delivery oversight.

(b) **Gabriel J Perez Irizarry (CTO)** — Shall be responsible for technology operations, sales, partnerships, administration, and acting as the Company's registered agent in Puerto Rico.

**5.3 Voting.** Except as otherwise provided in this Agreement, decisions requiring a vote of the Members shall be determined by a majority of the ownership interests. Each Member's voting power shall be proportional to their ownership interest.

**5.4 Major Decisions.** The following actions shall require the unanimous consent of all Members:

(a) Sale, transfer, or disposition of all or substantially all Company assets;
(b) Merger, consolidation, or conversion of the Company;
(c) Admission of new Members;
(d) Amendment of this Operating Agreement;
(e) Incurring debt obligations exceeding $50,000;
(f) Entering into contracts with a total value exceeding $100,000;
(g) Changing the Company's principal office or registered agent;
(h) Filing for bankruptcy or voluntary dissolution of the Company.

**5.5 Banking.** The Company shall maintain one or more bank accounts in the name of the Company. Both Managing Members shall be authorized signers on all Company bank accounts.

---

## ARTICLE VI — COMPENSATION AND EXPENSES

**6.1 Member Compensation.** Members may receive reasonable compensation for services rendered to the Company, as agreed upon by the Members.

**6.2 Reimbursement.** Members shall be reimbursed for reasonable expenses incurred on behalf of the Company in the ordinary course of business, provided such expenses are properly documented.

---

## ARTICLE VII — TRANSFER OF MEMBERSHIP INTERESTS

**7.1 Restrictions on Transfer.** No Member may sell, assign, transfer, pledge, or otherwise dispose of all or any portion of their membership interest without the prior written consent of the other Member(s).

**7.2 Right of First Refusal.** If a Member desires to transfer their interest and receives a bona fide offer from a third party, the remaining Member(s) shall have a right of first refusal to purchase the interest on the same terms and conditions as the third-party offer, exercisable within thirty (30) days of written notice.

**7.3 Involuntary Transfer.** In the event of death, disability, or bankruptcy of a Member, the remaining Member(s) shall have the option to purchase the affected Member's interest at fair market value, as determined by an independent valuation.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — DISSOLUTION

**8.1 Events of Dissolution.** The Company shall be dissolved upon:

(a) The unanimous written consent of all Members;
(b) The entry of a decree of judicial dissolution under applicable law;
(c) Any event that makes it unlawful for the Company to continue its business.

**8.2 Winding Up.** Upon dissolution, the Company's affairs shall be wound up by the Members, who shall:

(a) Pay or adequately provide for all debts, liabilities, and obligations of the Company;
(b) Distribute the remaining assets to the Members in proportion to their respective ownership interests.

---

## ARTICLE IX — BOOKS, RECORDS, AND TAX MATTERS

**9.1 Books and Records.** The Company shall maintain complete and accurate books and records of account, which shall be kept at the Company's principal office and be available for inspection by any Member at reasonable times.

**9.2 Fiscal Year.** The fiscal year of the Company shall be the calendar year.

**9.3 Tax Returns.** The Company shall file all required federal, Puerto Rico, and local tax returns. The Company shall file IRS Form 1065 (U.S. Return of Partnership Income) annually, with the first filing due March 15, 2026.

**9.4 Tax Matters Partner.** **Gabriel J Perez Irizarry** shall serve as the Tax Matters Partner (or Partnership Representative, as applicable) for purposes of federal and Puerto Rico tax proceedings.

---

## ARTICLE X — INDEMNIFICATION

**10.1 Indemnification.** The Company shall indemnify and hold harmless each Member and their agents from and against any claims, liabilities, damages, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from their good faith actions on behalf of the Company, except for acts of willful misconduct, fraud, or gross negligence.

---

## ARTICLE XI — DISPUTE RESOLUTION

**11.1 Mediation.** In the event of any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement or the affairs of the Company, the Members shall first attempt to resolve the matter through good faith negotiation. If the dispute is not resolved within fifteen (15) days, the Members shall submit the matter to mediation administered by a mutually agreed-upon mediator in San Juan, Puerto Rico.

**11.2 Binding Arbitration.** If mediation fails to resolve the dispute within thirty (30) days of commencement, the dispute shall be submitted to binding arbitration in San Juan, Puerto Rico, conducted in accordance with the rules of the American Arbitration Association. The decision of the arbitrator shall be final and binding upon the Members and may be enforced in any court of competent jurisdiction.

**11.3 Costs.** The costs of mediation and arbitration shall be shared equally by the Members, unless the arbitrator determines otherwise. Each Member shall bear their own attorneys' fees unless the arbitrator awards fees to the prevailing party.

**11.4 Continued Operations.** During any dispute resolution process, the Members shall continue to perform their respective duties and obligations under this Agreement, and the Company shall continue its normal business operations to the extent practicable.

---

## ARTICLE XII — MISCELLANEOUS

**12.1 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement among the Members with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, and negotiations.

**12.2 Amendments.** This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by all Members.

**12.3 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, including Act No. 55-2020 (Puerto Rico Civil Code) and applicable LLC statutes.

**12.4 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

**12.5 Notices.** All notices required or permitted under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered to the Members at their addresses on file with the Company.

**12.6 Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.
